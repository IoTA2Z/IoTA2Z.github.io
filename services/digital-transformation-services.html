Hardware Architecture and Design Process

While hardware design has long utilized a waterfall-style engineering methodology, the fast-paced, ever-changing, cost-competitive nature of the world of IoT has taught us that some of the same Agile principles that have revolutionized software development can and often must be successfully applied (with some modification) to the IoT hardware design and development process.

Of course software and hardware development are quite different. As a result, applying Agile development to hardware requires a few basic parameters. First, everyone needs to be on the same team to see plans, progress and calculate the physical effects of one part on the rest. The high-costs associated with hardware development means it is especially important to have transparency. Altering a product once the architectural decisions are completed does not mean erasing a few bits of code and rewriting them. Instead, you may have to order new parts, apply heat to weld or add more microchips. All these add expenses and end up affecting both the project timeline and cost. Applying Agile development processes will inevitably lower the price for hardware devices and result in more profit for your company and/or lower prices for your customers.

While most would argue the refinement of Agile for hardware development is still a work in progress, IOTA2Z has found that IoT project success is optimized though the use of a modified variant of Agile, including features such as:

  • A focus on iterative development and rapid prototyping, while embracing changing requirements during the process.
  • Using longer sprint periods to accommodate the timeframes typically associated with even granular hardware development tasks.
  • Focusing on more modular hardware designs where some sprints might focus on a particular module, while other sprints might focus on integrating multiple modules.
  • Realizing that delivering a customer-presentable product at the end of each sprint is often not practical or cost prohibitive, and therefore replacing such sprint deliverables with virtual simulations of functionality which still can provide strong visibility into project progress and requirements alignment.
  • IOTA2Z will work with you to develop an optimal and fully-transparent hardware design process that will best optimize your project success.
